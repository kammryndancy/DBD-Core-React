How to Resolve Differences in Your Bank Statement

 

As you are reconciling your bank statement, your goal is to bring the values in the Working Totals section and Amount to Reconcile field to zero. If these figures are not at zero, but you feel that you have cleared all of the necessary items, please follow these instructions to find where the issue is.

 

If Working Totals are not reduced to zero:

 

  1. Run the Reconciliation Report.
  2. You will need to check your deposit entries, check entries, adjustments, and NOF transactions.
  3. Check that all items were entered with the correct sign. For example, using the "minus" sign as needed.
  4. Check that all items were entered for the correct amount.
  5. Check that all of the information from the bank statement was entered correctly from the start.
  6. If any errors are found in steps 3, 4, or 5, you will need to return to the Bank Statement Data Processing and re-enter the data.

 

If the Amount to Reconcile is not reduced to zero:

 

  1. Print or attain copies of the following reports for the period that you are reconciling:
  • AR Cash Receipts Journals
  • AP Cash Disbursements Journals
  • AP Manual Check Journals
  • GL Detail Transaction Registers
  1. Look over your Cash Receipts Journals. Were all of the deposits processed and updated for the period end balance? If not, update all of the Cash Receipts data, and recheck the entries.
  2. Look over your Cash Disbursements and Manual Check Journals. Were all of the check runs processed and updated for the period end balance? If not, update all of the AP data, and recheck the entries.
  3. Look over your GL Detail Transaction Register. Was this run and updated after all check runs and deposits were processed? If not, re-run the Detail Transaction Register and verify the new ending GL balance.
  4. Were any transactions included in the Bank Rec that were dated for another period? If so, delete them from the Bank Rec and recheck the totals.
